About Me

I’m an undergraduate Physics student at St. Xavier’s College with a growing passion for Astrophysics, cosmology, and applied research. My internships at SAC-ISRO and in space technology and rocketry introduced me to satellite data analysis, remote sensing workflows, propulsion concepts, and orbital mechanics, and these areas continue to shape how I think about space systems. I enjoy working through complex problems, experimenting with new tools, and staying detail-oriented in projects that demand precision and curiosity. I’m motivated by learning through practice and exploring ideas that connect theory with real-world applications. Outside academics, I often turn to photography, especially landscapes and astrophotography, as a way to slow down and observe the world more closely.

Research & Projects

  • TEC Prediction with GNSS–Aditya-L1 & LSTM; Space Applications Centre (ISRO): Developed an LSTM-based TEC forecasting model using GNSS VTEC and Aditya-L1 solar data, performing STEC to VTEC processing, filtering, feature engineering, and low-error predictions in a full Python workflow.
  • Extended Kalman Filter for GPS Positioning; Space Applications Centre (ISRO): Developed an EKF framework for GPS navigation across multiple dynamics, including state/measurement modelling, clock-error handling, noise covariances, and Newton/Gauss-Newton initialization.
  • Rocket Physics, Propulsion & GMAT Orbital Simulations; STAR: Worked with propulsion relations, ascent dynamics, mass–staging analysis, and orbital calculations, and ran GMAT simulations for circular and transfer orbits.
  • Study of the Doppler Effect; St. Xavier’s College: Simulated Doppler shifts with a rotating sound source, comparing measured and theoretical frequencies with <1% error, and presented the results.
  • Quantum Wavefunction Tunneling & Trapping; St. Xavier’s College: Simulated Gaussian wave packets in a 1D finite well using Crank–Nicolson TDSE, showing tunneling, reflection, and trapping, and analyzing bound-state occupations via eigenstate projections.
  • Mechanical Equivalent of Heat (J) Using Thermocouple-Arduino; St. Xavier’s College: Built an Arduino–thermocouple setup to measure temperature rise during electrical heating and compute J=E/Q, including signal conditioning, calibration, and full data-analysis workflow.
  • Analysis of Resonance in a Series LCR Circuit; St. Xavier’s College: Measured current across varying AC frequencies to identify the resonance peak and confirm impedance minimization through experimental plots.
